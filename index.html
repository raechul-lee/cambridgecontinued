THE JOURNEY OF Q U I N T U S

He was the son of a dead man. His father, Lucius Caecilius Iucundus, had met his fiery demise at the hands of Mount Vesuvius. His mother was killed somewhere else in Pompeii like a mere commoner. His home, his city, his life was utterly destroyed. His name was Q u i n t u s .

Quintus Caecilius Iucundus escaped Pompeii by a stroke of luck. His numerous escapades in the Roman world blurred together, but he vaguely remembered traveling to Egypt, finding a long lost son, and intercepting a plot against some king. Cogidubnus? No, Cogidubstep. Ah, yes.

In the present, Quintus is in a bit of a predicament. He is in an unfamiliar land, on what appears to be an island of rolling hills and snow-capped mountains along with lush greenery and a rugged coastline. How did he get here? Well, all he remembers is lying in his bed in Britain and suddenly seeing a bright flash before his eyes. When he got up, he was on this strange island. Quintus gets up and starts walking. There must be people in this beautiful place, he thinks. He sees a sign in his tongue. "Formosa," it reads. Beautiful land? Hmm, makes sense.
